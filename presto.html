Redesigning Presto

I took it upon myself to redesign Presto’s website out of frustration.

The idea of a single card that can be used as many times as you feel fit, not having to worry about getting the full use of a monthly or yearly pass, or adding funds from the convenience of your own home are all a huge advantage and a step in the right direction for making a commuter’s life a little easier. Presto has the right idea, but they’re just not fulfilling their true potential. I’ve been using Presto since 2011 and I’d like to help by solving a few problems that have made me mildly infuriated these past five years.

First of all, their website is a complete disaster. Commuters are not sitting in front of a desktop at home or the office. They’re either sitting or waiting for the bus. This means they’re most likely on a mobile device. Presto’s website should be catering to mobile users, actually, in general it should be made more convenient to log in, view, navigate and, make transactions no matter what device you’re viewing the website on.

“One tap and a car comes directly to you. Your driver knows exactly where to go. And payment is completely cashless.”

This is Uber’s statement, it seems like Presto and Uber have something in common. However, Uber is actually doing things right, providing a convenient, prompt and cashless user experience. User experience is what Uber has and what Presto is lacking. Acquiring positive user experience is what will help Presto achieve its full potential.

Comparing Presto to Uber

Presto’s page contains an abundance of useless information like, “ Enter your username and password to log into your online account.” This is just redundant information, do you really need to explain this a user who has clicked the “Log In” button from the front page? The header already says “Account Log In.”

An orange information box, which isn’t consistent with any of the site’s colours contains outdated information. Can’t this information be moved to a more appropriate area of the website?

I just want to log in, not search a wall of text for a tiny input field. Why is the anonymous log in an option? A user who has registered isn’t even able to use this to access their account. “…login anonymously with limited functionality.” What does this even mean? At least provide useful information if you’re going to add any at all.

Lastly, does the “Forgot Password/username” need to be separate links? Why does Presto insist on using a username? Usernames are for forums, just have the user provide their email address for authentication. Simple and easy to remember. Passwords on the other hand can be forgotten, so provide a single “Forgot Password” link.

Aligned, top to bottom, showing only what’s necessary making it easy for the user to intuitively understand the steps to log in.

  • Link back to the front page
  • What is the user here for?
  • Clearly labeled input fields that don’t require squinting
  • A convenient “Remember Me” option for frequent visitors
  • A single link for the forgetful
  • A large button to complete the log in
  • Sign up link
